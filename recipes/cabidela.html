Arroz de Cabidela

One of the oldest and most know protuguese recipes

Ingredients
  • 100g of onion
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 4 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 400g of tomato
  • 750g of chicken
  • water
  • 1 tablespoons of salt
  • 300g of rice
  • 2 tablespoons of vinegar
Preparation
  1. Sauté the onion and the chopped garlic cloves and bay leaf in a pan with oil.
  2. Peel the tomato, clean it of seeds, chop into small pieces and add to the sauté. Let it cook, stirring occasionally until the tomato is tender.
  3. Add the chicken cut into pieces, season with 1 tablespoon of salt and cook, with the pan covered, for about 10 minutes over moderate heat.
  4. Add hot water to cover the chicken, cover it again and simmer gently for another 20 minutes. Add a little more boiling water so that you have about three and a half times the volume of rice. Adjust the salt, if necessary, and as soon as the boiling resumes, introduce the rice. Stir and cover.
  5. After 13 minutes, add the blood, previously mixed with the vinegar. Mix well and boil another 1 to 2 minutes. Remove from heat and serve immediately. Serve with salad.
